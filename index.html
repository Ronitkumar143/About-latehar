Latehar, Jharkhand – A Hidden Gem of Natural Beauty

Latehar, a picturesque district in Jharkhand, is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, dense forests, and rich tribal culture. Located in the Palamu division, it is a nature lover’s paradise, offering stunning waterfalls, lush greenery, and diverse wildlife. One of Latehar's prime attractions is Betla National Park, home to tigers, elephants, deer, and a variety of birds. This park is part of the Palamu Tiger Reserve, making it a significant destination for wildlife enthusiasts. Another major highlight is Lodh Falls, the highest waterfall in Jharkhand, cascading from a height of about 143 meters and offering a mesmerizing view. Latehar is also famous for Netarhat, a serene hill station often called the "Queen of Chotanagpur". Its cool climate, scenic sunrise and sunset points, and tranquil environment make it a perfect getaway. Upper Ghaghri Falls, another natural wonder, adds to Latehar’s charm. Apart from its natural beauty, Latehar has a rich tribal heritage. The region is home to several indigenous communities that preserve their unique traditions, folk dances, and festivals. With its blend of wildlife, waterfalls, hills, and culture, Latehar remains one of Jharkhand’s most captivating destinations, waiting to be explored.

Top Attractions

  • Betla National Park: A famous wildlife sanctuary with tigers, elephants, and deer.
  • Lodh Falls: One of the highest waterfalls in Jharkhand.
  • Netarhat: A scenic hill station known as the 'Queen of Chotanagpur'.
  • Upper Ghaghri Waterfalls: A beautiful waterfall near Netarhat.
